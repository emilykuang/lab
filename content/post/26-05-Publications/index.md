Our lab has seven new publications this semester, thanks to all our collaborators and students for their hard work and contributions! A common theme across these works is human-AI collaboration and visual analytics tools across different contexts, including UX evaluation, sensemaking, software engineering, and presentation authoring.

One full paper that I presented at CHI 2026 in Barcelona: 
- [**“It Became My Buddy, But I’m Not Afraid to Disagree”: A Multi-Session Study of UX Evaluators Collaborating with Conversational AI Assistants**](/lab/publication/chi26-longitudinal-ux)

Two workshop papers at CHI 2026: 
- [**"I''m Not Reading All of That": Understanding Software Engineers'' Level of Cognitive Engagement with Agentic Coding Assistants**](/lab/publication/chi26w-se-cognitive-engagement)
- [**Beyond One User and One Model: Rethinking LLMs as Mediators in Collaborative Sensemaking and Ideation**](/lab/publication/chi26w-co-data)

Two articles in the International Journal of Human-Computer Interaction: 
- [**Standardizing the Evaluation of Usability Test Results: Criteria Development and Human-AI Collaborative Performance**](/lab/publication/ijhci26-standardizing-quality/)
- [**MultiUX: A Human-AI Collaborative Tool to Facilitate Multiple Usability Test Video Analyses**](/lab/publication/ijhci26-multiux)

Two papers to be presented at DIS 2026 in Singapore:
- [**MindTrellis: Co-Creating Knowledge Structures with AI through Interactive Visual Exploration**](/lab/publication/dis26-mindtrellis)
- [**When Constraints Limit and Inspire: Characterizing Presentation Authoring Practices for Evolving Narratives**](/lab/publication/dis26-reslide)
